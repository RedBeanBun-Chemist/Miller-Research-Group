Papers accepted, awards received, talks given, and updates from our work in flow chemistry, photocatalysis and molecular imaging.

2026

Jan 2026

Defluoroalkylation of HCF₃ published in Angew. Chem.

In collaboration with the Crimmin group, we report a method for converting trifluoromethane — a potent greenhouse gas with 14,600× the warming potential of CO₂ — into valuable fluoroalkene building blocks using organolithium reagents. The paper also elucidates the mechanism by which defluoroalkylation proceeds.

Publication Angew. Chem. Int. Ed. 2026, 65 (1) →
Jan 2026

Open-source M-Arc photoreactor published in React. Chem. Eng.

Rowan and Mónica lead a paper describing the M-Arc — a modular, 3D-printed photoreactor that delivers 3.25× more photons than comparable commercial systems. All CAD files and build instructions are freely available, so that any lab can reproduce the setup at a fraction of the cost.

Publication React. Chem. Eng. 2026, 11, 228–233 →

2024

Continuous flow defluorosilylation of HFC-23 and HFO-1234yf

Published in Organic Letters with the Crimmin group, this work translates our fluorine-upgrading chemistry into a safe, continuous-flow process — demonstrating how flow technology enables otherwise hazardous fluorine chemistry to be carried out reproducibly.

Publication Org. Lett. 2024, 26, 8605–8609 →

Photoredox-catalysed functionalisation of strained heterocycles

Our collaborative study in J. Org. Chem. with the Bull group and Pfizer develops a radical functionalisation of benzylic oxetanes and azetidines using visible-light photoredox catalysis — addressing a long-standing gap in methods for incorporating four-membered polar motifs in medicinal chemistry.

Thermal stability assessment of diazo compounds

Published in Organic Process Research & Development with the Bull group, this study systematically assesses the thermal stability and explosive hazard of commonly used diazo compounds and diazo-transfer reagents — a practical resource for process chemists working with these reactive intermediates.

Publication Org. Process Res. Dev. 2020, 24, 67–84 →
