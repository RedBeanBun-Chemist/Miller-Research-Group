Join the Group

We welcome enquiries from prospective PhD students, postdoctoral researchers, MRes and MSci project students who want to work at the interface of synthesis, engineering and medicine.

Why the Miller Group

What you'll work on

Projects in the group sit at the meeting point of three worlds — synthetic chemistry, reactor engineering, and translational healthcare. You'll learn to think about reactions not only as transformations but as processes — to build the kit that runs them, and to evaluate them against real-world constraints of safety, cost and scalability.

01

Real chemistry, real reactors

Students design and build flow setups, photoreactors and microfluidic devices in-house. Our open-source M-Arc photoreactor and continuous-flow HCF₃ platform are good examples of what a typical group project looks like in practice.

02

Industry-embedded projects

Many projects are co-supervised with industrial partners including BASF, GSK, Pfizer, AstraZeneca, Almac and others through the IConIC Prosperity Partnership and the React CDT. You'll spend time on site, present to industrial stakeholders, and see your chemistry evaluated at pilot scale.

03

A distinctive skill stack

Miller Group alumni leave with a combination of synthetic skill, hands-on engineering experience and process-safety awareness that is genuinely rare. Graduates move into pharma process development, CDMOs, academic postdocs, and start-ups building continuous-manufacturing technology.

PhD · Funded

Novel carbonylation-homologation strategies for peptide bond formation

Co-supervised with BASF · IConIC Prosperity Partnership · UK residents particularly welcome

Peptides are among the most important emerging classes of therapeutic molecule, but their synthesis still relies on stoichiometric coupling reagents that generate large volumes of waste. This project will develop a fundamentally different approach — using catalytic carbonylation to form amide bonds directly from amines and carbon monoxide — and translate it into continuous flow for scalable, atom-economical peptide assembly.

What we look for

The kind of person who does well here

We care more about curiosity and tenacity than CV points. Students who thrive in the group share three traits:

  1. They want to build things. You'll spend real time in SolidWorks, writing Python for kit, and assembling tubing. If that sounds like a waste of a chemist, this is the wrong group.
  2. They read widely. The best projects start at the interface of two fields neither supervisor is an expert in. Reading outside chemistry is a habit we actively encourage.
  3. They write clearly. A paper that communicates a clever result badly is worth less than a clean one reporting something smaller. Writing is treated as a core skill from day one.

A strong undergraduate record in chemistry, chemical engineering, or a related discipline is expected. Prior hands-on experience with NMR, HPLC or flow chemistry is welcome but not required — we train you.

How to apply

Send Philip an email

Before applying to any formal programme, please get in touch with Philip directly. Including the items below in your first email significantly speeds things up.

1
A short cover note — two paragraphs on what you want to work on and why the Miller Group specifically. Generic "I am interested in your research" messages are not competitive.
2
Your CV — one page is fine; academic records, research experience, and any relevant technical skills.
3
Transcripts — undergraduate (and Master's if applicable). Unofficial copies are fine for a first enquiry.
4
Funding plans — which CDT, scholarship, or funding source you intend to pursue. If you're not sure, say so — we'll help you work it out.
5
Names of two referees — not reference letters, just names and email addresses at this stage.
