PhD · Funded

Novel carbonylation-homologation strategies for peptide bond formation

Co-supervised with BASF · IConIC Prosperity Partnership · UK residents particularly welcome

Peptides are among the most important emerging classes of therapeutic molecule, but their synthesis still relies on stoichiometric coupling reagents that generate large volumes of waste. This project will develop a fundamentally different approach — using catalytic carbonylation to form amide bonds directly from amines and carbon monoxide — and translate it into continuous flow for scalable, atom-economical peptide assembly.

Standing invitations

  • Postdoctoral researchers — if your profile aligns with our research themes, we are always happy to discuss fellowship applications (Marie Skłodowska-Curie, Royal Society, EPSRC, 1851 Commission, Imperial College Research Fellowships).
  • MRes / MSc project students — we regularly host projects linked to the React CDT and the ICB CDT. Enquire at least two terms in advance.

How PhDs are funded

Most of our PhD students come through one of the routes below. Each has a different timeline and different residency requirements — check the individual programme pages carefully.

Imperial President's PhD Scholarships

Highly competitive fully-funded scholarships open to applicants of any nationality. A strong route for exceptional candidates approaching us independently rather than via a CDT.

Scholarship page →

China Scholarship Council

Joint Imperial–CSC scholarships cover tuition and stipend for Chinese-national applicants. Annual call opens around January; early contact with Philip is essential.

Scholarship page →

Industrial CASE & partnership studentships

Projects co-funded by BASF and other IConIC partners, typically advertised on FindAPhD. These come with a strong industrial component and usually a placement.

Self-funded

We consider self-funded applicants (personal, government scholarships, third-party) on the same basis as any other. Please include funding plans in your initial email.
