Philip Miller is an Associate Professor in Applied Synthesis within the Department of Chemistry at Imperial College London. His research group operates at the intersection of chemistry, engineering, and medicine — developing innovative approaches in catalysis, flow chemistry, and microfluidics to tackle challenges in chemical synthesis and medical imaging.

Philip obtained his BSc and PhD from Queen's University of Belfast. In 2004, he joined Imperial College as a postdoctoral researcher, where he pioneered the development of microfluidic reactors for radiolabelling applications. He was subsequently awarded a prestigious EPSRC Life Sciences Interface Fellowship (2007–2011), held jointly at Imperial College and Aarhus University PET Centre in Denmark.

His research programme spans organometallic chemistry, photocatalysis, and reactor engineering. Recent work has included the development of sustainable methods for repurposing greenhouse gases such as trifluoromethane (HCF₃), the design of open-source 3D-printed photoreactors, and catalytic carbonylation strategies for peptide synthesis.

Philip is a co-investigator within the IConIC Prosperity Partnership, a £17.8M EPSRC programme led by BASF and Imperial College London aiming to transform the manufacture of high-value chemicals through continuous flow processes.
