About

Dr Philip W. Miller

Philip Miller is an Associate Professor in Applied Synthesis within the Department of Chemistry at Imperial College London. His research group operates at the intersection of chemistry, engineering, and medicine — developing innovative approaches in catalysis, flow chemistry, and microfluidics to tackle challenges in chemical synthesis and medical imaging.

Philip obtained his BSc and PhD from Queen's University of Belfast. In 2004, he joined Imperial College as a postdoctoral researcher, where he pioneered the development of microfluidic reactors for radiolabelling applications. He was subsequently awarded a prestigious EPSRC Life Sciences Interface Fellowship (2007–2011), held jointly at Imperial College and Aarhus University PET Centre in Denmark.

His research programme spans organometallic chemistry, photocatalysis, and reactor engineering. Recent work has included the development of sustainable methods for repurposing greenhouse gases such as trifluoromethane (HCF₃), the design of open-source 3D-printed photoreactors, and catalytic carbonylation strategies for peptide synthesis.

Research

Research Themes

Our work integrates synthetic chemistry with engineering and digital technologies to develop practical solutions for chemical manufacturing and healthcare.

Flow Chemistry

Developing continuous flow methods for scalable, safe chemical synthesis — from organolithium reactions and hazardous intermediates to telescoped multi-step processes. Our work within the IConIC Prosperity Partnership aims to transform chemical manufacturing.

Homogeneous Catalysis

Investigating transition metal–catalysed transformations including carbonylation reactions, cross-coupling methods, and novel catalytic strategies for sustainable C–C and C–N bond formation.

Photocatalysis

Harnessing visible light for radical-mediated transformations via photoredox catalysis. Our open-source 3D-printed M-Arc photoreactor delivers reproducible photochemistry accessible to all researchers.

Microfluidics & Reactor Design

Designing and fabricating microfluidic reactor systems for precise parameter control, enhanced safety with energetic materials, and rapid reaction optimisation using inline analytics.

Coordination Chemistry

Synthesising and studying novel metal complexes — including unusual Pd–Zn clusters, phosphine-substituted organolithium reagents, and fluorinated organometallics — to unlock new reactivity.

Radiolabelling & PET Imaging

Developing radiolabelling strategies for Positron Emission Tomography using carbon-11 and fluorine-18 tracers, combining flow microreactor technology with medical imaging applications.

IConIC Prosperity Partnership

The Miller Group is a co-investigator within the Innovative Continuous Manufacturing for Industrial Chemicals (IConIC) programme — a £17.8M EPSRC Prosperity Partnership led by BASF and Imperial College London. The partnership aims to transform the manufacture of high-value chemicals through continuous flow processes.
